Barbie in Real Life

Elizabeth Blackwell



Elizabeth Blackwell was the first female doctor in the United States of America. She started pursuing this career after being told by friends how humiliating seeing male doctors was. However, due to her gender she got rejected from many medical universities, except Genova Medical College in New York. Despite opposition from not only other students but also professors, Blackwell graduated at the top of her class in 1849. She was able to travel to Europe after earning her medical degree and perform many successful surgeries, until she contracted an eye disease causing her to lose sight in her left eye. This disease stopped Blackwell from being able to perform any more surgeries. However, she was able to start her own practice in New York as well as a women's medical school and two clinics for impoverished women and children.

bell hooks



bell hooks, the pen name for Gloria Jean Waltkins, is best known for her works about race, feminism, and class. Hooks was an American author, theorist, educator, and social critic from Hopkinson, Kentucky. Her writing explored the intersectionality of race, gender, capitalism, and their ability to produce and preserve oppression and domination. With over 40 published books, hooks wrote for a wide audience with works including essays, poetry, and even children's books. Apart from creating change and awareness with her writing, hooks also taught different studies and subjects at various Colleges. She taught ethnic studies at the University of Southern California, African and Afro-American studies at Yale university, women’s studies at Oberlin College, and English at the City College of New York. In 2004, hooks became a professor at Berea College in Kentucky, where later the bell hooks Institute was founded.

Vigdís Finnbogadóttir



Vigdís Finnbogadóttir is an Icelandic politician who became the first female president and fourth president of Iceland. She was the world’s first woman president and served for 16 years (1980-1996), making her also the longest-serving female head of state of any country. Before being appointed president, Finnbogadóttir taught French drama at the University of Iceland before becoming the Artistic Director of the Reykjavík Theatre Company. In 1996, she was the founding chair of the Council of Women World Leaders at Harvard University. 2 years later she became president of the United Nations Educational, Scientific, and Cultural Organization. She is currently a UNESCO Goodwill Ambassador and member of the Club of Madrid.

Michelle Obama



Michelle Obama has been inspiring and empowering women long before becoming the first lady. She started out as an assistant commissioner at City Hall in Chicago before eventually becoming vice president of community and external affairs at the Medical Centre in the University of Chicago. Later in 2015, she and her husband Barack Obama launched Let Girls Learn. It encouraged girls to stay in school and asked the government to make greater investments towards their education. Her actions being done with passion to break down stereotypes and remove the barriers for girls’ education. She also started her Let’s Move initiative, urging for healthier food choices for kids. Also, her and current First Lady Jill Biden launched Joining Forces to support members of the armed forces, veterans, and their families. Her history of taking practical steps to make social changes, specifically for women, has made her a role model to many.

Laura Bassi



Laura Bassi was the first female to earn a doctorate in science and the first salaried female professor. Bassi had no formal education from school and was privately tutored from age 5 to 20. She became well versed in major subjects, such as sciences and mathematics. She was awarded a doctoral degree on May 12, 1732 after defending 49 theses in front of professors at the University of Bologna. A month later Bassi was appointed by the university as its first female professor, with the restriction of not being able to teach all-male classes. She became Italy’s most important populariser of Newtonian mechanics. And in 1745 she was inducted by the Pope to the Benedettini, a group of research scientists. She took up the Chair of Experimental Physics in 1776, where she stayed until her death.

Ruth Handler



Ruth Handler is best known as the inventor of Barbie. Her husband Elliot Handler and her worked together to form the toy company Mattel. What started in their garage would later become a billion dollar company. The idea of Barbie started in 1951 with a child’s doll with an adult body and multiple outfits. But while vacationing in Switzerland with her daughter Barbara they were transfixed by a window display of dolls in different ski outfits. 3 years later, in 1959, Barbie dolls were put on the market. However when she started getting ill with Breast Cancer Handler was forced out of the company she had started. But this led to her company Nearly Me. The company was made to manufacture a more realistic version of a woman’s breast, for others like Handler who had lost theirs. Handler’s company and Barbie invention continue to be important for women of all ages around the world.
